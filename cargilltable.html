Family Table Priority Framework

Cross-Platform AI Analysis Model

The analysis centers on the idea that family systems work best when relationship roles and influence are clear. This framework organizes participants into three tiers of proximity to the children and the Cargill family unit.

1. The Family Table Model

Tier 1 — Primary Family (Seats at the Table)

These individuals have direct responsibility and lifelong connection to the children.

Examples

  • Parents
  • Step-parents in the household
  • The children themselves

Role

  • Direct parenting authority
  • Decision-making responsibility
  • Emotional and physical care of children
  • Primary communication channel

These individuals sit at the table where decisions are made.

Tier 2 — Close Kin (Standing Near the Table)

These individuals are family or strongly connected relatives.

Examples

  • Grandparents
  • Siblings
  • Aunts / uncles
  • Cousins
  • Long-term partners integrated into the family

Role

  • Supportive influence
  • Advisory or emotional support
  • Not primary decision makers

They support the table, but do not control the table.

Tier 3 — External Adults (Outside the Table)

These individuals do not have family or blood ties to the family unit.

Examples

  • Friends
  • Acquaintances
  • External adults
  • Partners not part of the family structure

Role

  • Limited or indirect involvement
  • Should not influence parenting decisions
  • Communication influence should be minimal

They exist outside the table, meaning they should not shape family dynamics involving the children.

Defined Project Parameters

  • Role clarity
  • Influence alignment
  • Boundary integrity

  • Escalation patterns
  • Role confusion
  • Child impact assessment

  • Top five risks

2. Framework Objective for AI Analysis

Each AI platform should evaluate the dataset with the following goals:

  • Identify communication patterns
  • Identify role confusion
  • Identify influence imbalance
  • Identify triangulation
  • Identify boundary violations
  • Identify communication escalation
  • Provide child-centered recommendations

The analysis should determine whether communication patterns respect the Family Table model.

4. Prompt to Give All Three AI Platforms

You are analyzing a dataset of family communication involving co-parenting and related adults.

The analysis should use the "Family Table Priority Model".

Family Table Model:

Tier 1 – Primary family responsible for the children (parents, step-parents, children)

Tier 2 – Close kin related to the family (grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins)

Tier 3 – External adults with no family tie

Your task is to analyze communication patterns and determine whether communication influence aligns with appropriate family roles.

Focus on:
- role clarity
- communication boundaries
- triangulation
- escalation patterns
- child impact

Do not take sides or assign blame.

Provide a neutral, objective analysis centered on improving communication in the best interest of the children.

Follow the analysis questions provided.
